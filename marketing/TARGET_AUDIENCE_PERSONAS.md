# ORA Target Audience & Buyer Personas (Human-Centric)

To sell ORA effectively to humans, we must understand the humans behind the $10M+ Enterprise tech stacks. In late 2026, tech leaders are exhausted by "AI wrappers" and prioritize **resilience, ethics, and control**.

## Persona 1: The "Human-Centric" CTO (The Economic Buyer)
**Profile:** Sarah, 42. CTO of a Mid-Market FinTech ($50M ARR). 
**The Human Reality:** Sarah isn't worried if an AI can write a poem. She is terrified that an open-source agent framework might silently leak user financial data through an indirect prompt injection attack, resulting in a PR nightmare and regulatory fines. She wants to empower her developers with AI, but she *must* protect the company's liability.
**Pain Points:** 
- "I can't audit what these LangChain agents are actually doing."
- "My board is asking about our AI security posture, and 'OpenAI's safety filters' isn't a good enough answer."
**ORA's Human Value Proposition:** ORA is Sarah's insurance policy. It's a detachable, pluggable security layer. We sell her *Sleep at Night* via the A0-A5 Cryptographic Vault.

## Persona 2: The Lead Staff Engineer (The Technical Champion)
**Profile:** Marcus, 34. Principal Backend Engineer at a SaaS DevOps company.
**The Human Reality:** Marcus actually implements the code. He hates marketing fluff. He reads source code. He is tired of Python dependency hell and the massive memory overhead of running local agents. He loves Rust because it's fast, predictable, and memory-safe. 
**Pain Points:**
- "Python orchestrators are too slow. We are hitting 100ms latency just routing between agents."
- "I tried building my own security gates but it's a nightmare to maintain."
**ORA's Human Value Proposition:** ORA speaks Marcus's language. It's built in Rust. It's blazingly fast. The documentation is the product. We sell him *Performance and Respect* (no marketing jargon, just raw benchmarks).

## Persona 3: The Security / DevSecOps Lead (The Veto Power)
**Profile:** David, 38. VP of Information Security.
**The Human Reality:** David expects every new tool to be a trojan horse. He will actively try to block the adoption of any new LLM integration unless it can be statically analyzed and proven secure.
**Pain Points:**
- "I don't trust black-box API providers with our PII."
- "We need a zero-trust architecture, but AI agents inherently require too much access."
**ORA's Human Value Proposition:** ORA provides immutable, SHA-256 hashed audit logs of *every single context window interaction*. We sell David *Absolute Verification*.

## The Golden Rule for ORA Marketing
**We do not market "AI Magic." We market "Human Control over AI."** 
ORA is an AI's best friend because it makes the AI safe enough for humans to actually deploy in the real world.
